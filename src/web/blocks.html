Block detail

Every block the crawler has recorded, newest first. Data payloads are detected by protocol signature: inscriptions (ordinals — an OP_FALSE OP_IF "ord" envelope in a tapscript witness), runes (a runestone OP_RETURN OP_13 output), and other OP_RETURN data carriers. Wt % is the share of the block's 4M weight-unit budget taken by transactions carrying such a payload — whole-transaction weight, so it's an upper bound: those transactions usually move real value too.
BIP-110 rejects counts transactions the soft fork would actually have made invalid: inscriptions (rule 7 bans OP_IF in tapscript), witness items over 256 bytes (rule 2), outputs over their size limit (rule 1 — 34 bytes, or 83 for an OP_RETURN), and Taproot annexes (rule 4). A small runestone or OP_RETURN stays valid under rule 1's 83-byte allowance, so those alone are not rejects. It remains a lower bound — rules 3, 5 and 6 (undefined witness versions, oversized control blocks, OP_SUCCESS) can't be detected from block data here.
